Percentage Calculator – Complete Guide, Formulas & Examples

A percentage calculator helps you calculate percentages, percentage increases, percentage decreases, discounts, ratios, and percentage changes instantly. Percentages are used in business, finance, education, statistics, marketing, investments, taxes, and everyday shopping decisions.

Whether you need to calculate exam scores, salary increases, retail discounts, profit margins, tax rates, or business growth, this free percentage calculator provides fast and accurate results.

What Is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. The symbol (%) means "per hundred." Percentages make it easier to compare values and understand proportions.

Examples:

Percentage Meaning
25% 25 out of 100
50% Half of a whole
100% The complete amount

Percentage Formula

The standard percentage formula is:

Formula Type Formula
Basic Percentage (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
Percentage Increase ((New − Old) ÷ Old) × 100
Percentage Decrease ((Old − New) ÷ Old) × 100
Discount Calculation Original Price × (Discount % ÷ 100)

How to Calculate Percentage

To calculate a percentage:

  1. Identify the part value.
  2. Identify the whole value.
  3. Divide the part by the whole.
  4. Multiply the result by 100.

Example: If you scored 45 marks out of 60:

(45 ÷ 60) × 100 = 75%

((12,000 − 10,000) ÷ 10,000) × 100 = 20%

Methodology & Accuracy

All calculations use standard mathematical formulas as defined in international mathematics curricula and financial standards. Percentage of a number follows the formula P/100 × N; percentage change uses ((New − Old) / Old) × 100 per standard statistical practice. Results are computed in JavaScript's 64-bit IEEE 754 floating-point arithmetic and rounded to 4 significant decimal places to minimise floating-point drift.
